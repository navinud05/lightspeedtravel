10:00 AM
Depart Home
Seattle: South via Tacoma → Hwy 3 → US-101 to Hoodsport (~2.5hrs).
Portland: North via I-5 → Olympia → US-101 to Hoodsport (~3hrs).
12:30 PM
Meet at Hoodsport — Last Services
Fuel up, grab ice, last cell service. Rally point: Hoodsport gas station on US-101. Air down tires (25-30psi for forest roads). Portland crew may arrive ~1:00.

Head West — Enter Forest Roads
From Hoodsport, head west on Skokomish Valley Road → forest road network toward Three Peaks Loop. Transition from pavement to gravel. ~30-40min to trail start.

Full loop. Mixed terrain — gravel forest roads, rocky sections, moderate elevation changes through second-growth and old-growth forest. Pace for scenery, stop at viewpoints. ~2.5-3hrs at a relaxed pace.
5:30 PM
Make Camp — Dispersed
Finish the loop and find a dispersed pulloff in the surrounding NF road network. Or head to FR-25 (Hamma Hamma) for river-adjacent camping (~30min east).

💡 Option B Advantages

  • Relaxed Day 1: No pressure to rush into the trail after a long drive. Arrive, set up, socialize.
  • Morning trail run: Three Peaks Loop in cool morning light = better conditions, better photos.
  • Rigs tested: Camp access on Day 1 lets everyone shake out their setup before the real trail.
  • Flexibility: If someone has a vehicle issue on Day 1, you catch it before committing to the loop.

🎒 Overland Packing List

🛻 Vehicle & Recovery

  • Tire deflator & portable compressor
  • Recovery boards (Maxtrax or similar)
  • Tow strap + soft shackles
  • High-lift or bottle jack + base plate
  • Tire repair kit (plug kit + spare)
  • Bow saw or chainsaw (downed trees)
  • Basic tools, zip ties, hose clamps
  • Extra fuel — 2-5gal can
  • FRS/GMRS radios (1 per truck)
  • 12V fridge or quality cooler

🏕️ Camp

  • 5-gal water jug (no potable water)
  • Tent + rain fly + ground tarp
  • Sleeping bag (rated 35-40°F)
  • Sleeping pad
  • Camp stove + fuel
  • Fire pan (check burn bans)
  • Firewood (buy at stations)
  • Chairs + table
  • Lantern + headlamps
  • Trowel + WAG bags

🥾 Hike & Explore

  • Rain jacket (ALWAYS)
  • Layers: base + fleece + shell
  • Waterproof boots or trail runners
  • Daypack, water, snacks
  • Tide table printout
  • Binoculars (whales, elk)
  • Camera
  • Bear hang kit

📋 Nav & Admin

  • OnX Offroad — offline maps downloaded
  • Gaia GPS backup
  • USFS MVUM printed
  • NPS Pass ($30 or $80 annual)
  • Discover Pass ($35/yr)
  • Cash for campgrounds
  • NO cell service most areas
  • Itinerary shared with non-attendee
