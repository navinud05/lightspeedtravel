Converge & Overland — Three Peaks Loop

Seattle/Portland → Hoodsport → Three Peaks Loop → Camp
🚗 ~3.5 hrs (incl. trail)
~120mi paved + ~25mi dirt
🛤️ Overland 🌲 Forest
~Noon
Depart Home
Seattle: South via Tacoma → Hwy 3 → US-101 to Hoodsport (~2.5hrs).
Portland: North via I-5 → Olympia → US-101 to Hoodsport (~3hrs).
~2:30 PM
Meet at Hoodsport — Last Services
Fuel up, grab ice, last cell service. Rally point: Hoodsport gas station on US-101. Air down tires here if desired (30-35psi for forest roads).

Head West — Enter Forest Roads
From Hoodsport, head west on Skokomish Valley Road → forest road network toward Three Peaks Loop staging area. Transition from pavement to gravel. ~30-40min to trail start.
3:30–6:00 PM

Run the full loop. Mixed terrain — gravel forest roads, rocky sections, moderate elevation changes through second-growth and old-growth forest. Pace for scenery, not speed. Stop at viewpoints.
6:30 PM
Make Camp — Dispersed
Finish the loop and find a dispersed pulloff in the surrounding NF road network. Multiple options along the forest roads radiating from the loop. Alternatively, head to FR-25 (Hamma Hamma) for river-adjacent camping.

Hurricane Ridge — Alpine Summit

Camp → Hoodsport → US-101 North → Port Angeles → Hurricane Ridge
🚗 ~3.5 hrs total
~120 miles
⛰️ Mountains 🛤️ Forest Roads
~11:00 AM
Break Camp → Air Up → Head to US-101
Navigate back to pavement. Air tires back up to highway pressure (35-38psi). Head north on US-101 along Hood Canal — scenic waterfront highway.
1:00 PM
Port Angeles — Lunch & Fuel
Last real town until Forks. Fuel up, grab food. Good spots: Next Door Gastropub, New Day Eatery, Sabai Thai. ~2hrs from camp.
2:00 PM
Hurricane Ridge Road (17mi, 45min climb)
Sea level to 5,242ft. Switchbacks, increasingly dramatic views. Stop at pullouts — each one better than the last. NPS entry fee ($30/vehicle or pass).
2:45 PM
Hurricane Ridge Summit
360° panorama: Olympic peaks, Strait of Juan de Fuca, Vancouver Island on clear days. Wildflower meadows in full bloom. Marmots sunning on rocks.
3:00–5:00 PM
Hurricane Hill Trail (3.2mi RT, 700ft gain)
Best alpine hike on the peninsula. Paved start → dirt path to a 360° summit. Snow-capped peaks in every direction. Friday = full trail access (spur closed Mon-Thu for construction in 2026). Allow 1.5-2hrs.
5:30 PM
Descend — Scout Camp
Head back down. Options: dispersed on forest roads south of PA (Little River Rd / FR-2880) or camp at elevation if Deer Park Road has opened (check at visitor center).
6:30 PM
Make Camp
Little River Road area offers dispersed pulloffs in second-growth forest. Quiet, off the main highway, ~15min from PA.

🏕️ Night 2 Camp

Dispersed — Little River Road / FR-2880 (south of Port Angeles)
Backup: Heart O' the Hills Campground (NPS, first-come, $20) — 5mi up Hurricane Ridge Rd
📍 ~48.02, -123.50 (Little River Rd dispersed area)

Hoh Rainforest — Deep Green

Port Angeles → Lake Crescent → Forks → Hoh Rainforest
🚗 ~2.5 hrs total
~95 miles
🌲 Rainforest 🛤️ Forest Roads
~Noon
Break Camp — Head West on US-101
Scenic drive along Lake Crescent. One of the most beautiful highway segments in WA — turquoise glacial lake, old-growth shoreline.
12:45 PM
Lake Crescent — Marymere Falls (optional)
Quick stop: 1.8mi RT hike to 90ft waterfall through old growth. Or just pull over and admire the lake. 45min if hiking.
2:00 PM
Forks — Quick Resupply
Gas, ice, supplies at Thriftway. Last proper town for 2 days. Stock up for camp tonight and tomorrow.
2:30 PM
Upper Hoh Road → Hoh Rain Forest VC
18mi paved road into the heart of the rainforest. Increasingly lush — ferns, moss, massive trees close in. NPS entry fee.
3:15 PM
Hall of Mosses Trail (0.8mi loop)
THE Olympic Peninsula experience. Cathedral arches of bigleaf maple draped in club moss. Sitka spruce with 30ft buttress roots. Absolutely silent. 30-45min.
4:00 PM
Spruce Nature Trail (1.2mi loop)
Loops to the Hoh River. Roosevelt elk common in the meadows — scan clearings. Some of the largest trees you'll ever see.
5:00 PM
Optional: Hoh River Trail (go as deep as you want)
The trail continues 17mi to the glaciers. Even 1-2mi beyond the visitor center offers increasing solitude and primeval forest.
5:30 PM
Exit → FS-29 (Sitkum-Sol Duc Road)
Back to 101 → east of Forks → turn onto FS-29. Gravel forest road with multiple dispersed camping pulloffs. First 5-6mi are accessible to all trucks.
6:15 PM
Make Camp — FS-29
Pick a pulloff. Sites are flat, forested, well-established. Room for multiple rigs. Fire rings often present.

🏕️ Night 3 Camp

Dispersed — FS-29 (Forest Service Road 29), east of Forks
Backup: Bogachiel State Park (first-come, $25) or Hoh Campground (NPS, $20)
📍 ~47.92, -124.22 (FS-29 dispersed pulloffs, first 5mi)

🛻 Overland Notes — Day 3

  • FS-29 exploration: If the group has energy, the road continues deeper and gets rougher. Good scouting for more remote sites.
  • Further down FS-29 (mile 6+) requires higher clearance — good adventure if rigs are capable
  • Elk are EVERYWHERE in this area at dusk — drive carefully

Pacific Coast — Rialto to Kalaloch

Forks → Rialto Beach → Ruby Beach → Kalaloch → Quinault
🚗 ~2.5 hrs total
~90 miles
🌊 Pacific Coast
~11:00 AM
Break Camp — Head to Rialto Beach
Slightly earlier start to maximize coast time. FS-29 → Forks → La Push Road → Mora Road. ~30min to trailhead.
11:30 AM
Rialto Beach → Hole-in-the-Wall
3mi round trip beach hike. Massive Pacific driftwood, sea stacks, thundering surf. Hole-in-the-Wall sea arch at 1.5mi — passable at mid-low tide only. Check tide tables! Allow 2+ hours for exploring.
1:30 PM
Drive South — US-101 Coastal Stretch
Through Forks and south on 101. This section of highway runs right along the coast with multiple beach pulloffs.
2:30 PM
Ruby Beach
Crown jewel of the Olympic coast. Sea stacks, Abbey Island, crashing Pacific waves. 0.2mi trail to beach. Otherworldly in fog or sun. Spend an hour minimum.
3:30 PM
Beach 4 + Kalaloch
Tide pools at low tide (starfish, anemones). Find the "Tree of Life" just north of Kalaloch Lodge — massive Sitka spruce clinging to an eroded cliff by its roots. Quick roadside stops.
4:30 PM
Continue South to Quinault
~45min on US-101 south to the Lake Quinault / Amanda Park area.
5:15 PM
South Shore Road — Gravel Transition
Turn onto South Shore Road at Lake Quinault. Pavement gives way to gravel as you pass Gatton Creek. Dispersed sites along the river on NF-2292.
5:45 PM
Make Camp — Final Night
River-adjacent pulloffs under towering conifers. Quinault River rushing nearby. Last night vibes — big fire, good conversation, stars.

🏕️ Night 4 Camp

Dispersed — South Shore Road / NF-2292, past Gatton Creek
Backup: Gatton Creek Campground (first-come, $20) or Kalaloch CG (NPS, reservable)
📍 ~47.455, -123.735 (South Shore Rd dispersed pulloffs)

🌊 Tide Alert — Day 4

  • Hole-in-the-Wall at Rialto: Only passable at tides below ~5ft. Check NOAA tide charts for La Push station.
  • Tide pools at Beach 4/Kalaloch: Best at low tide (exposed reef).
  • Plan your timing around tides — check NOAA La Push Tides

🎒 Overland Packing List

🛻 Vehicle & Recovery

  • Tire deflator & portable compressor (air down/up daily)
  • Recovery boards (Maxtrax or similar)
  • Tow strap + soft shackles
  • High-lift jack or bottle jack + base plate
  • Tire repair kit (plug kit + spare)
  • Bow saw or small chainsaw (downed trees on forest roads)
  • Basic tool kit (wrenches, zip ties, hose clamps)
  • Extra fuel — 2-5gal can (limited gas stations)
  • FRS/GMRS radios (1 per truck minimum)
  • 12V fridge or quality cooler with block ice

🏕️ Camp

  • 5-gal water jug (NO potable water at dispersed sites)
  • Tent + rain fly + ground tarp (June = still rainy)
  • Sleeping bag (rated 35-40°F)
  • Sleeping pad
  • Camp stove + fuel (primary cooking — check fire bans)
  • Fire pan if running campfires (LNT in dispersed sites)
  • Firewood (buy at ranger stations, DON'T forage)
  • Camp chairs + table
  • Lantern + headlamps
  • Trowel + WAG bags (pack out waste at dispersed)

🥾 Hike & Explore

  • Rain jacket (always — this is the Olympic Peninsula)
  • Layers: base + fleece + shell (50s–70s, colder at ridge)
  • Waterproof hiking boots or trail runners
  • Daypack with water + snacks
  • Tide table printout (Rialto Beach access)
  • Binoculars (whales, elk, eagles)
  • Camera (sea stacks demand it)
  • Bear canister or hang kit (food storage at camp)

📋 Navigation & Admin

  • OnX Offroad app — offline maps downloaded for Olympic NF
  • Gaia GPS or equivalent as backup
  • USFS Motor Vehicle Use Map (MVUM) — printed
  • National Parks Pass ($30/vehicle or $80 annual)
  • Discover Pass for WA state land ($35/year)
  • Cash (some campgrounds cash-only)
  • NO cell service in most locations — plan meetup/rally points in advance
  • Written itinerary shared with someone not on the trip (safety)
